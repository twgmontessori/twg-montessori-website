What to Expect at a Montessori School Visit

Visiting a Montessori school gives families the opportunity to experience the classroom environment, meet educators, ask questions, and understand how Montessori learning comes to life. Knowing what to expect can help you feel prepared, relaxed, and ready to make the most of your visit.

Before You Arrive

A little preparation helps your visit feel calm and productive.

Before your school visit, confirm the appointment time, review the school’s website, prepare your questions, and ask whether your child should attend.

📅

Confirm the Appointment

Double-check the date, arrival time, location, parking information, and approximate length of the visit.

📝

Prepare Your Questions

Bring a short list of questions about teachers, curriculum, routines, safety, communication, fees, and enrollment.

👶

Ask Whether Children Should Attend

Some visits are designed for adults, while others may include an opportunity for children to see the classroom or meet educators.

⏰

Arrive on Time

Arriving a few minutes early helps the visit begin smoothly and shows respect for the school’s daily schedule.

Arrival & Welcome

Your visit usually begins with an introduction to the school.

A staff member may greet you, explain the visit format, review the school’s philosophy, and share basic information about the program before the tour begins.

👋

Meeting the School Team

You may meet the director, administrator, classroom educator, or another team member who will guide the visit.

🌱

Introduction to Montessori

Expect a brief explanation of the Montessori philosophy, prepared environment, work cycle, and child-centered approach.

📚

Program Overview

The school may explain the age group served, program hours, daily schedule, curriculum areas, and enrollment process.

🧭

Visit Expectations

You may be asked to observe quietly, avoid interrupting children, or save detailed questions until after the classroom visit.

Classroom Observation

Observation helps families understand Montessori in action.

During the classroom portion of your visit, notice how children choose work, move through the space, use materials, interact with educators, and care for the environment.

👀

Engaged Children

Look for children who appear focused, comfortable, independent, and meaningfully involved in their activities.

🧩

Purposeful Materials

Notice whether Montessori materials are organized on low shelves and available for children to choose independently.

🤝

Teacher Guidance

Observe whether educators guide calmly, offer lessons respectfully, and allow children space to practice independently.

🧘

Classroom Atmosphere

A Montessori classroom may be active, but it should also feel orderly, respectful, purposeful, and emotionally safe.

Exploring the School Environment

A school visit often includes more than the classroom.

Families may also tour shared spaces, outdoor areas, entrances, rest spaces, washrooms, and other parts of the facility.

🌿

Outdoor Space

Ask how outdoor play is scheduled, supervised, and supported throughout Vancouver’s changing weather.

🛡️

Entrances & Security

Notice how visitors enter, how children are signed in and out, and how authorized pick-up is managed.

😴

Rest Areas

Learn how sleeping children, quiet resters, and non-nappers are supported during the day.

🧼

Care Spaces

Observe whether washrooms, handwashing areas, meal spaces, and storage areas are child-accessible and organized.

Meeting the Educators

Conversations with educators help families understand the school’s approach.

During or after the tour, you may have an opportunity to learn about teacher qualifications, classroom guidance, communication, and how educators support individual children.

👩‍🏫

Teacher Qualifications

Ask about Montessori training, ECE certification, classroom experience, and ongoing professional development.

👀

Observation & Individual Support

Learn how educators observe children’s interests, readiness, development, and progress over time.

🤝

Positive Guidance

Ask how teachers respond to conflict, strong emotions, separation, and challenging moments.

💬

Family Communication

Learn how families receive updates, share concerns, and stay connected with their child’s classroom experience.

Asking Questions

The best visits leave room for thoughtful conversation.

After observing the school, take time to ask about the details that matter most to your family, including routines, expectations, communication, safety, fees, and enrollment.

📅

Daily Routine

Ask about arrival, the work cycle, meals, outdoor play, rest, enrichment activities, and dismissal.

🩺

Health & Safety

Ask about illness, allergies, medication, emergency procedures, supervision, and visitor access.

💳

Fees & Enrollment

Review tuition, additional fees, deposits, enrollment requirements, withdrawal policies, and available spaces.

🎒

Transition Support

Ask how the school helps new children separate from caregivers, learn routines, and feel secure.

After the Visit

Take time to reflect before making your decision.

After the tour, review your notes and consider how the school’s philosophy, environment, educators, routines, and community align with your child’s needs and your family’s values.

📝

Review Your Notes

Write down your impressions while the visit is still fresh, including both strengths and remaining questions.

❤️

Consider the Overall Feeling

Think about whether the school felt warm, respectful, calm, organized, and genuinely child-centered.

☎️

Follow Up

Contact the school if you need clarification about policies, enrollment, availability, or your child’s individual needs.

✅

Understand the Next Step

The next step may include submitting an application, joining a waitlist, completing forms, or arranging another visit.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about Montessori school visits.

What happens during a Montessori school visit?

A visit may include an introduction to the program, classroom observation, a tour of the school, conversations with educators, time for questions, and an explanation of the next steps.

How long does a Montessori school visit take?

The length varies by school. Confirm the approximate duration when booking so you can allow enough time for observation and questions.

Can I observe the Montessori classroom?

Many schools include classroom observation, but the format varies. Visitors may be asked to observe quietly and avoid interrupting children.

Should I bring my child?

Ask the school in advance. Some visits are designed for adults, while others may include an opportunity for children to attend.

What should I do after the visit?

Review your notes, consider the overall fit, follow up with questions, and confirm the application, waitlist, or enrollment process.
