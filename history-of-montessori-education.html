Montessori History

The History of Montessori Education

From a small classroom in Rome to thousands of schools around the world, discover how Montessori education has inspired generations of children, families, and educators for more than a century.

Where Montessori Began

A new way of understanding children.

The history of Montessori education begins with Dr. Maria Montessori, an Italian physician, scientist, and educator who believed that children deserved to be understood with respect and careful observation.

At the beginning of the 20th century, many classrooms were based on memorization, discipline, and teacher-directed instruction. Dr. Montessori took a different approach. She watched how children moved, explored, repeated activities, solved problems, and learned from their environment.

Her work led to a revolutionary idea: children are not empty vessels waiting to be filled with knowledge. They are naturally curious, capable learners who develop best when given freedom within a prepared environment.

The First Children's House

The Casa dei Bambini opened in 1907.

In 1907, Dr. Montessori was invited to work with children in San Lorenzo, a working-class neighbourhood in Rome. The classroom became known as the Casa dei Bambini, or Children's House.

Instead of forcing children to sit still and follow rigid lessons, Dr. Montessori prepared a calm, orderly environment with child-sized furniture and purposeful learning materials. She allowed children to choose meaningful work, repeat activities, and move freely within clear limits.

The results surprised many observers. Children who had been seen as difficult or undisciplined became focused, independent, careful, and joyful in their work. This first Children's House became the beginning of the Montessori Method.

A GLOBAL MOVEMENT

How Montessori education spread around the world.

The success of the first Children's House quickly attracted attention from educators, scientists, and governments. Teachers from many countries travelled to Italy to observe Dr. Montessori's classrooms and learn her methods firsthand.

Within just a few years, Montessori schools began opening across Europe, North America, Asia, South America, and beyond. Dr. Montessori also travelled extensively, delivering lectures and training teachers who would introduce the Montessori Method to new communities around the world.

Books such as The Montessori Method were translated into many languages, helping families and educators understand her educational philosophy. By the early twentieth century, Montessori education had become an international movement rather than a single school in Rome.

CHALLENGES AND PERSEVERANCE

Montessori education continued despite difficult times.

As Montessori education spread internationally, the world entered a period of political unrest and two World Wars. During these challenging years, many Montessori schools faced closures, and educational priorities changed in different countries.

Dr. Maria Montessori continued travelling, teaching, and training educators whenever possible. During World War II, she spent several years in India, where she trained hundreds of teachers and further developed her ideas about peace education and global citizenship.

Rather than allowing conflict to change her vision, Dr. Montessori became even more convinced that education could help create a more peaceful world. She believed that children who grow up with respect, independence, responsibility, and empathy would become adults capable of building a better future.

These ideas remain an important part of Montessori education today, where classrooms encourage cooperation, respect for others, and appreciation for different cultures.

MONTESSORI TODAY

A global educational movement that continues to grow.

More than one hundred years after the first Children's House opened in Rome, Montessori education continues to thrive across the world. Today, Montessori schools can be found in more than 140 countries, serving children from infancy through adolescence.

While each Montessori school reflects its local culture and community, they all share the same core principles: respect for the child, carefully prepared environments, hands-on learning, independence, and freedom within clear boundaries.

Modern Montessori classrooms continue to use many of Dr. Montessori's original learning materials while also embracing contemporary knowledge about child development. Research in psychology and neuroscience has supported many of her observations about how children learn through movement, exploration, repetition, and meaningful experiences.

Families continue to choose Montessori because it helps children develop not only academic knowledge, but also confidence, responsibility, concentration, creativity, compassion, and a lifelong love of learning.

THE FUTURE OF MONTESSORI

Building on more than a century of educational innovation.

Montessori education continues to evolve while remaining true to the principles established by Dr. Maria Montessori more than one hundred years ago. Around the world, educators continue to refine classroom practices, incorporate new research, and respond to the changing needs of children and families.

Although technology and society have changed dramatically since 1907, children's developmental needs remain remarkably consistent. They still learn best through meaningful relationships, hands-on experiences, movement, independence, and opportunities to make purposeful choices.

These timeless principles explain why Montessori education continues to attract families who want more than academic success. They want their children to become confident, compassionate, responsible, and lifelong learners who are prepared for an ever-changing world.

At Together We Grow Montessori School, we are proud to continue this tradition by providing an authentic Montessori environment where children can develop the knowledge, character, and independence they need for the future.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about the history of Montessori education.

When was Montessori education founded?

Montessori education began in 1907 when Dr. Maria Montessori opened the first Casa dei Bambini (Children's House) in Rome, Italy.

Where did Montessori education begin?

Montessori education began in the San Lorenzo district of Rome, Italy, where Dr. Maria Montessori created the first Children's House.

Why did Dr. Montessori create the Montessori Method?

Through scientific observation, Dr. Montessori discovered that children learn best through independence, movement, and hands-on experiences.

How did Montessori education spread around the world?

Teachers travelled to Italy to learn from Dr. Montessori, and her books, lectures, and training programs helped establish Montessori schools in more than 140 countries.

Is Montessori education still popular today?

Yes. Montessori education continues to grow worldwide and is practiced in thousands of schools serving children from infancy through adolescence.
