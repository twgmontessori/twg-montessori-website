Admissions

Begin Your Family’s Montessori Journey

Choosing the right preschool is an important family decision. Our admissions process is designed to be warm, thoughtful, and transparent—from your first inquiry through the school tour, family interview, admission decision, and enrollment.

Program Eligibility

Who Our Casa Program Is Designed For

Together We Grow offers a full-day mixed-age Montessori Casa experience. Before beginning the admissions process, families are encouraged to review the core program requirements below.

Ages 2.5–5

Children must be between 2.5 and 5 years of age at the time of enrollment.

Toilet Trained

Children are required to be toilet trained before admission and ready to manage basic toileting routines with age-appropriate guidance.

Full-Day Program

The regular Montessori program operates from 8:30 a.m. to 5:00 p.m., with optional extended-care services.

Family Partnership

We seek families who value respectful communication, consistent attendance, and the Montessori approach to independence and learning.

Step by Step

Our Admissions Process

Each stage helps families understand our school and helps us thoughtfully consider how we can support each child within the classroom community.

Submit an Inquiry

Tell us about your family, your child, your desired start date, and whether you are interested in a school tour, the waitlist, or both.

Admissions Follow-Up

Our admissions team reviews your information and will normally contact you within 1–2 business days with suitable next steps.

Visit the School

Families are invited to learn about the Casa environment, daily rhythm, Montessori philosophy, and expectations for family partnership.

Family Interview

Before a decision is made, we meet with the family to learn more about the child, discuss goals and expectations, and answer questions.

Admission Decision

The school considers program eligibility, space availability, the interview, classroom balance, desired start date, and overall program suitability.

Complete Enrollment

After a place is offered, families complete the enrollment package and submit all required supporting documents and fees.

Before Our Decision

The Family Interview

The interview is a thoughtful conversation—not a test. It gives our school and your family an opportunity to learn more about one another before an admission decision is made.

We may discuss your child’s interests, routines, previous group experiences, independence, communication, health or support needs, and your hopes for a Montessori education.

Participation in an interview does not guarantee admission. Decisions remain subject to available space, program suitability, classroom balance, and final school approval.

Learn About Your Child We want to understand your child as an individual, including strengths, interests, routines, and areas where support may be helpful.
Discuss Montessori Expectations Families can learn more about independence, consistent attendance, the uninterrupted work cycle, outdoor participation, and respectful classroom routines.
Answer Family Questions The interview is also your opportunity to ask detailed questions about the program, communication, transition, and school-family partnership.
Consider Mutual Fit Our goal is to determine whether the program can appropriately support the child and whether the school’s philosophy aligns with the family’s expectations.

Enrollment Considerations

Building a Stable Classroom Community

Admission is based on availability and thoughtful consideration of the classroom community. Priority may be given to the following families.

Returning Students & Siblings

Continuity supports children, families, and the stability of the mixed-age Montessori community.

Full-Time Enrollment

Our full-day program and consistent attendance support the rhythm, relationships, and uninterrupted learning of the Casa environment.

Montessori Alignment

We value families who understand and support Montessori principles, respectful partnership, independence, and consistent school routines.

Space Availability & Classroom Balance

Decisions also consider available space, age balance, desired start date, program suitability, and the needs of the mixed-age classroom community.

Priority considerations do not guarantee admission. Final decisions are also influenced by available spaces, age balance, desired start date, program suitability, and the needs of the classroom community.

After an Offer

Completing the Enrollment Package

When a place is offered, families receive the enrollment package and supporting school documents. Enrollment is not confirmed until all required items have been received and approved.

Required Forms & Records

  • Enrollment Application Form
  • Authorized Pick-Up Information
  • Medical and Allergy Information Form
  • Childcare Emergency Consent Form
  • Immunization record or signed exemption documentation
  • Photo and Media Consent Form
  • Parent Handbook Acknowledgement

Fees, Selections & Preparedness

  • Non-refundable registration fee
  • Security deposit equivalent to one month’s tuition
  • Optional early drop-off, late pick-up, and lunch selections
  • Pre-Authorized Debit form, when applicable
  • Labeled Emergency Comfort Bag
  • Any individual medical or support information requested by the school

When a Space Is Not Immediately Available

Families may choose to join the waitlist. A waitlist application does not guarantee enrollment. We will contact families when a potentially suitable space becomes available and explain the next stage of the admissions process.

Admissions FAQ

Questions Families Often Ask

What ages does Together We Grow accept?

Our full-day Montessori Casa program is designed for children ages 2.5 to 5 at the time of enrollment.

Does my child need to be toilet trained?

Yes. Children must be toilet trained before admission and able to manage basic toileting routines with age-appropriate guidance from educators.

Is the family interview required?

Yes. The interview takes place before our admission decision and helps the school and family discuss the child, Montessori expectations, family goals, and overall program fit.

Does attending a tour or interview guarantee admission?

No. An inquiry, tour, interview, or waitlist application does not guarantee admission. Enrollment remains subject to available space, program suitability, classroom balance, and final approval.

What are the regular and extended-care hours?

The regular program operates from 8:30 a.m. to 5:00 p.m. Optional early drop-off is available from 7:30–8:30 a.m., and optional late pick-up is available from 5:00–6:00 p.m., subject to registration and availability.

When is my child’s enrollment officially confirmed?

Enrollment is confirmed only after the school approves the placement and receives all required forms, supporting documents, the registration fee, and the required security deposit.

What happens if there is no space available?

You may choose to join the waitlist. The school will contact you if a potentially suitable space becomes available, but waitlist placement does not guarantee enrollment.
