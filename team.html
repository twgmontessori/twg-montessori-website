A Letter from Our Founder

Some journeys begin with a textbook. Mine began with watching my two sons grow.

Raised in a Montessori environment, my boys showed me something no classroom theory could fully teach: when a child is trusted, respected, and given the freedom to explore, they do not simply learn. They flourish.

My path in education has been shaped by two worlds. Growing up and studying in China, then building my career and family in Canada, I came to believe that the very best educators draw from the richness of many cultures and bring that wisdom into their care for children.

Together We Grow was founded with one clear intention: to create a place where every child feels seen, capable, and inspired.

My Montessori Belief

Children do not need adults to direct every step of their learning. They need a thoughtfully prepared environment, meaningful freedom, respectful guidance, and time to develop concentration, independence, confidence, and a genuine love of discovery.

I believe children deserve more than a place to spend their day. They deserve a beautiful, purposeful environment where their independence is respected, their curiosity is protected, and their growing confidence is supported by caring adults who truly know them.

Together We Grow is my heart’s work. Every detail of the school is being developed with care: from the prepared classroom and Montessori materials to the daily rhythm, family communication, and the educators who will become part of this community.

Our name reflects what I believe most deeply. Children grow, but so do parents, educators, and communities. When we learn from one another and work in genuine partnership, we create something far greater than a school.

Warmly,Connie ChanFounder & Principal

How We Teach

The values we expect every educator to share.

01

Observe Before Intervening

We watch carefully, seek to understand, and offer guidance that respects each child’s developmental readiness and growing independence.

02

Guide with Respect

Educators model calm communication, grace and courtesy, consistency, and respect in every interaction with children and families.

03

Grow in Partnership

Children thrive when families and educators communicate openly, share insight, and work together with trust and a common purpose.
