Parent Success Guide

Preparing for Montessori Preschool

Starting preschool is an exciting milestone for both children and parents. This guide will help your family prepare for a smooth, confident transition into Montessori life by building independence, establishing routines, and understanding what to expect during your child's first days at Together We Grow Montessori School.

Preparing for Success

Small routines at home can make the first days feel easier.

Before preschool begins, families can help children feel confident by practicing independence, predictable routines, self-care, and gentle separation.

🧥

Build Independence

Encourage your child to try simple self-help skills such as putting on shoes, carrying a backpack, washing hands, and managing personal belongings.

🚽

Support Toileting Confidence

Children should be toilet trained before enrollment and able to manage basic toileting needs with gentle guidance when needed.

🎒

Practice Daily Responsibility

Let your child help pack their bag, choose weather-appropriate clothing, clean up after activities, and care for simple classroom-like materials at home.

🌙

Create Healthy Routines

A consistent bedtime, calm morning routine, nutritious breakfast, and regular attendance help children feel secure and ready to participate.

❤️

Prepare Emotionally

Talk positively about school, practice short separations, and reassure your child that their teachers will help them feel safe and cared for.

🤝

Partner with the School

Share important information about your child’s routines, health, comfort needs, and development so educators can support a smooth transition.

Before the First Day

Help your child build confidence through simple daily practice.

Montessori preschool preparation is not about academics. It is about helping children feel capable, safe, and ready to participate in daily routines.

🧼

Handwashing

Practice washing hands before meals, after toileting, and after outdoor play. This supports health, independence, and daily classroom routines.

👟

Shoes and Outdoor Clothing

Encourage your child to try putting on shoes, jackets, and outdoor clothing. Weather-appropriate clothing helps children participate comfortably in outdoor play.

🍽️

Mealtime Independence

Let your child practice opening containers, drinking from a cup, using utensils, and cleaning up after snacks or meals.

🎒

Backpack Routine

Help your child recognize their backpack, place items inside, and carry it independently when arriving at school.

🧸

Comfort and Reassurance

Talk warmly about school and practice short goodbyes. A calm, confident parent helps children feel secure.

📅

Consistent Attendance

Regular attendance and predictable arrival routines help children settle into the Montessori work cycle and classroom rhythm.

What to Bring

A prepared child feels comfortable, confident, and ready for the day.

Labeling belongings and preparing simple essentials helps children care for themselves and supports a smooth classroom routine.

👕

Extra Clothing

Provide an extra set of labeled clothing for spills, accidents, weather changes, or outdoor play.

🌧️

Weather-Appropriate Gear

Outdoor play is part of the daily program when weather permits, so children should arrive with suitable clothing for rain, cold, or warm conditions.

🏷️

Labeled Belongings

Label clothing, shoes, containers, and personal items so your child and teachers can identify them easily.

🥪

Food Containers

If your child brings food from home or participates in the lunch program, labeled containers help support safe and organized mealtimes.

🧺

Comfort Bag

Families may be asked to provide an Emergency Comfort Bag with simple comfort and emergency items as part of school preparedness.

📄

Required Forms

Complete all enrollment forms, emergency contacts, health information, allergy details, and authorized pick-up information before your child begins.

Arrival and Daily Rhythm

A calm, predictable start helps children settle into Montessori life.

Consistent arrival routines help children feel secure, enter the classroom with confidence, and fully participate in the Montessori work cycle.

⏰

Arrive on Time

Children are expected to arrive before 9:00 a.m. so they can fully participate in the morning Montessori work cycle.

✍️

Sign-In and Sign-Out

Children must be signed in and out daily by a parent, guardian, or authorized individual.

👋

Short Goodbyes

A warm, brief goodbye helps children understand that school is a safe place and that parents will return.

📞

Notify Absences

If your child will be absent or late, notify the school by 9:00 a.m. so teachers can plan the day appropriately.

Family Partnership

Communication helps teachers support your child’s transition.

Families know their children best. Sharing information about routines, health, allergies, comfort needs, and developmental milestones helps educators provide consistent, respectful care.

💬

Share Helpful Information

Let teachers know about your child’s routines, interests, fears, comfort items, language needs, or recent changes at home.

🩺

Health and Allergies

Inform the school of allergies, medical conditions, medications, dietary needs, or any individual care requirements before your child begins.

🤲

Positive Guidance & Respect

Teachers support children with calm redirection, clear expectations, emotional language, problem-solving, and respect for each child’s development.

🌱

Home and School Consistency

Children feel most secure when families and educators work together with shared expectations, patience, and encouragement.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about preparing for Montessori preschool.

How can I prepare my child for Montessori preschool?

Practice simple independence skills at home, such as handwashing, toileting, putting on shoes, carrying a backpack, cleaning up, and following calm daily routines.

Does my child need to be toilet trained?

Yes. Children are required to be toilet trained before enrollment and able to manage basic toileting needs independently, with gentle guidance from educators when needed.

What time should my child arrive?

Children should arrive before 9:00 a.m. so they can fully participate in the Montessori work cycle and begin the day calmly with their classroom community.

What should my child bring?

Families should prepare labeled belongings, extra clothing, weather-appropriate outdoor gear, required forms, food containers if needed, and any school-requested emergency comfort items.

How can I help with separation?

Speak positively about school, practice short separations, keep goodbyes warm and brief, and reassure your child that teachers will help them feel safe and cared for.
