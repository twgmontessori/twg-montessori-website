Montessori vs Daycare

Choosing between Montessori preschool and traditional daycare is an important early childhood decision. Both can provide care, but their goals, classroom experiences, and approach to learning can be very different.

Understanding the Difference

Care, learning, and the early years.

Traditional daycare primarily supports families by providing supervision, routines, safety, and play while parents are at work. Many daycare programs create warm and caring environments where children can socialize, rest, eat, and participate in group activities.

Montessori education is different because it is intentionally designed as an early learning environment. A Montessori classroom is carefully prepared with hands-on materials that help children build independence, concentration, problem-solving skills, language, mathematics, practical life skills, and respect for others.

At Together We Grow Montessori School in Vancouver, our full-day Casa program combines thoughtful care with authentic Montessori learning for children ages 2.5 to 5.

Side-by-Side Comparison

Montessori school vs traditional daycare.

Montessori Preschool

  • Child-centered learning
  • Individual learning pace
  • Hands-on Montessori materials
  • Prepared classroom environment
  • Long uninterrupted work periods
  • Focus on independence and concentration
  • Practical life, sensorial, language, math, and cultural studies

Traditional Daycare

  • Supervision and care routines
  • Group play and activities
  • More flexible daily structure
  • Toys and general learning materials
  • Frequent transitions
  • Focus on socialization and daily care
  • May or may not include a structured curriculum

Parent Questions

Frequently asked questions.

Is Montessori better than daycare?

It depends on what your family is looking for. Daycare provides supervision and care. Montessori preschool provides care within an intentional educational environment focused on independence, concentration, and hands-on learning.

Does Montessori still include play?

Yes. Montessori recognizes that children learn through meaningful activity. Children explore, move, communicate, create, and discover through purposeful work and social interaction.

Will Montessori prepare my child for kindergarten?

Yes. Montessori supports independence, communication, social confidence, early literacy, early math, practical life skills, and emotional readiness.
